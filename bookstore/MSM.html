Man’s Search for Meaning by Viktor Frankl is a powerful memoir and psychological work based on his experiences as a prisoner in Nazi concentration camps during World War II. The book argues that the primary human drive is not pleasure, but the search for meaning.

Book Description

Frankl observed that those who survived the camps often did so because they found purpose—through love, work, or the courage to endure suffering. He concludes that even in the most horrific conditions, individuals retain the freedom to choose their attitude and response.

What You Will Learn

  • Life asks the questions—we must answer through action and choice
  • He who has a why to live can bear almost any how
  • Meaning can be found in three ways:
    • Work – Doing something meaningful
    • Love – Caring deeply for another
    • Suffering – Finding purpose in pain by how we bear it
