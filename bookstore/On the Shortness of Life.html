On the Shortness of Life is a moral essay written by the Roman Stoic philosopher Seneca the Younger around 49 AD, addressed to his father-in-law, Pompeius Paulinus. Book Description

On the Shortness of Life is a concise yet powerful Stoic essay by Seneca the Younger, written around 49 AD to his father-in-law, Paulinus. Far from lamenting the brevity of life, Seneca argues that life is long enough if used wisely—our problem is not a lack of time, but our wastefulness of it

What You Will Learn

  • Life is long enough if used well
  • Time is your most valuable asset
  • Busyness ≠ productivity
  • Don’t delay life
  • The past is secure, the future uncertain
